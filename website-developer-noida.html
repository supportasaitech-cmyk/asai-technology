Choosing a website developer in Noida — the 7-point checklist.

By ASAI Technology · 17 July 2026 · 6 min read

Search "website developer in Noida" and you'll drown in agencies, freelancers, and marketplaces all promising the same thing. Prices for the same website range from ₹4,000 to ₹80,000. Here's how to tell them apart in one conversation.

The 7-point checklist

  1. Click their live work. Not screenshots — links. Open them on your phone. Slow, broken, or template-identical sites tell you your future.
  2. Ask: "Who owns the domain and source code?" The only acceptable answer is you. Developer-owned domains are the #1 hostage situation in this industry — leaving them means losing your web address.
  3. Get ALL recurring costs in writing. Domain (~₹1,000/yr, in your account), hosting (free–₹500/mo), and nothing else unless you choose a care plan. "Small monthly maintenance fee" without a number is a red flag with a bow on it.
  4. Ask the timeline for a first draft. "2–3 weeks for a draft" for a 3-page site means you're in a queue, not a process. Modern studios deliver a full draft in 24–48 hours because the work is systemised.
  5. Check THEIR OWN website on your phone. If a developer's own site is slow or clumsy on mobile, the conversation is over. It's their best foot forward.
  6. Ask what a text change costs after launch. Fair: free or trivial. Unfair: per-ticket billing designed to milk you.
  7. Notice how they explain things. Jargon-as-armor ("we'll leverage responsive frameworks with dynamic CMS integration") usually hides thin skills. People who understand deeply can explain simply.

Frequently asked questions

How much do website developers charge in Noida?

Independent studios in Noida/Delhi NCR charge ₹5,000–₹15,000 for professional business sites in 2026. Agency pricing for identical scope runs ₹30,000–₹1,00,000, mostly covering their overheads.

Freelancer vs agency vs studio — which is better?

Freelancers are cheapest but vanish-risk is real; agencies are reliable but slow and expensive; small studios sit between — direct access to the person building, with product-level discipline. Judge any of them by the 7 checks in this guide, not the label.

What should I check before paying a developer?

Live work you can click, who owns the domain and code, all recurring costs in writing, delivery timeline, mobile quality of their own site, what changes cost after launch, and whether they answer questions plainly.
