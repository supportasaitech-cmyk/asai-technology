Guide · For Doctors

A website for your clinic that actually brings patients.

By ASAI Technology · 17 July 2026 · 5 min read

When a patient hears your name — from a friend, a pharmacy, an insurance list — the next thing they do is Google you. What they find in those 10 seconds decides whether they call. If the answer is a directory listing surrounded by ads for other doctors, you're losing patients you had already earned.

Why directory listings aren't enough

Practo, Justdial and similar platforms are useful — but on their pages, your competitors are one tap away, often as paid placements floating above you. Your own website is the only place on the internet where you are the only doctor in the room. It ranks for searches of your name, and increasingly for "[your specialty] in [your area]".

The 5 sections patients actually look for

  1. Who you are: qualifications, years of experience, hospital affiliations — with a good photo. Trust is the entire product.
  2. What you treat: a plain-language list of conditions and procedures. This is also what Google matches to patient searches.
  3. Where and when: timings, address with an embedded map, parking note. The most-visited section on every clinic site.
  4. The clinic itself: 4–6 real photos. Patients decide comfort levels visually.
  5. One-tap contact: a call button and a WhatsApp button, always visible on mobile. Over 80% of your visitors will be on a phone.

What NOT to include: stock photos of smiling foreign models, auto-playing music, a "blog" you'll never update, and paragraphs of medical jargon. Every extra element is between the patient and the call button.

Getting found for "doctor near me"

Three things make Google show your site to nearby patients: a Google Business Profile linked to your website, structured data on the site telling Google you're a physician at this address (invisible to humans, gold to search engines), and a fast site — Google measurably ranks slow sites lower. This is standard technical work, not magic; ask whoever builds your site to show you all three. Our SEO explainer covers it in plain words.

A single-doctor site with everything above: ₹5,000–₹15,000 one-time, domain in your name (₹1,000/year), hosting free or nearly free. Be suspicious of monthly fees to "keep the site running" — see our full website cost breakdown.

Frequently asked questions

Yes — patients now Google a doctor before visiting. A website you own ranks for your name and specialty, shows timing/location/credentials, and works even when practo-style listings bury you under competitors' ads.

What should a doctor's website include?

Five things patients actually check: your qualifications and experience, conditions you treat, clinic timings and location with a map, photos of the clinic, and a one-tap way to call or WhatsApp.
